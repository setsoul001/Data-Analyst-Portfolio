# Internally Displaced Persons Analysis

## Overview
Internally Displaced Persons are persons or groups of persons who have been forced or obliged to 
flee or to leave their homes or places of habitual residence, in particular as a result of or in 
order to avoid the effects of armed conflict, situations of generalized violence, violations of 
human rights or natural or human-made disasters, and who have not crossed an internationally 
recognized State border.

## Key Findings
- Global displacement has more than tripled since 2009 and has not had a single down year since 2019.
- The burden is structurally shifting toward low income countries, which now host roughly seven in ten classified IDPs versus about half in 2009.
- Africa plus Asia drive the global numbers, while Europe's small but fast-growing share is a useful early-warning signal for emerging crises rather than a major share of volume.
