# Star Wars Survey Analysis

## Overview
This project analyses a FiveThirtyEight survey on the Star Wars franchise. 
The survey covered viewership of the movies, ranking of favourite movies, 
favourability of characters, and a fan trivia question — "Who shot first?". 
A total of 1,186 people responded, 936 of whom have seen at least one film 
and form the basis of this analysis.

## Files
- `Star Wars Survey.ipynb` — Jupyter notebook containing all analysis and visualizations
- `Star_Wars_Survey_Analysis.docx` — Written interpretation of findings
- `StarWars.csv` — Raw dataset from FiveThirtyEight

## Tools Used
- Python
- pandas
- seaborn
- matplotlib
- numpy

## Key Findings
- Episode V is the most watched and most loved film in the franchise
- Collectively, the original trilogy (Episodes IV–VI) has higher viewership and better rankings than the prequels (Episodes I–III)
- The six highest-rated characters are all from the original trilogy; Jar Jar Binks is the only character with a negative favourability score
- Older viewers (45+) engage strongly with the original trilogy but drop off significantly with the prequels
- Answering "Han shot first" is a strong indicator of deeper fan engagement
