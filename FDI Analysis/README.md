# Foreign Direct Investment Analysis

## Overview
Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) is a critical indicator of economic integration, capital flow, and cross-border investment. 
This project analyses the FAO FDI dataset which spans across 195 countries from 1990 to 2023. 
The analysis explores historical FDI inflows and outflows to identify global investment patterns, regional shifts, and country-level performance over time.
The raw CSV dataset contains extensive yearly records, making it difficult to efficiently compare countries, monitor changes across years, and derive meaningful insights through manual analysis alone. 
To address this, the project delivers an interactive Power BI dashboard that transforms the raw data into clear and dynamic visualizations.

## Key Findings
- The United States has been the largest contributor to cumulative FDI since 1990 and has maintained this for the bulk of the 33 years span. 
- China have risen from 11th in inflows to a global cumulative 2nd and a 21st in outflow to 4th in global cumulative.
- Europe has been a major regional investor and investment receiver since 1990 but has faced a downward trend since 2013.
- Asia has been the most improved region moving from the 3rd in inflows and outflows in 1990 and as at 2023 has become the highest investor and also investment receiver.
- Historically, FDI inflows and outflow from Food, Beverages and Tobacco have exceeded FDI inflows and outflows from Agriculture, Forestry and Fishing for the bulk of the 33 years period.
